---

### "The LaTeX Trap"

Most researchers think they have a productivity problem.

They don't.

They have a _priority inversion_ problem.

Here's what I mean:

You spent 4 years learning differential equations.
3 years mastering your research domain.
2 years designing experiments.

And then you spend 40% of paper-writing time fighting with `\begin{figure}[htbp]`.

This is not a minor annoyance.

This is a **cognitive leak**.

Every hour you spend on syntax is an hour NOT spent on the work that actually matters - the ideas that could change your field.

The modern academic system profits from your distraction. Journals don't care if you spent 20 hours on formatting. They care about the 3 hours of insight buried in Section 4.2.

But here's what nobody tells you:

**The formatting problem is a solved problem.**

Not by "better templates."
Not by "learning LaTeX faster."
Not by Overleaf premium.

By letting machines do machine work.

---

### The Shift

AI isn't coming for researchers.

AI is coming for the _boring parts_ of research.

The parts you shouldn't be doing anyway.

Think about it:

- Citation formatting? Machine work.
- LaTeX syntax? Machine work.
- "Move figure to page 7"? Machine work.

Your job is not to be a typesetter.
Your job is to think thoughts nobody else is thinking.

The researchers who understand this will publish 3x more papers with half the effort.

The researchers who don't will keep copy-pasting between browser tabs.

### The System

I spent months building the exact system I wished existed.

It's called LMMs-Lab Writer.

Here's how it works:

1. Your files stay on YOUR machine. (No cloud lock-in. No subscription.)
2. AI tools access your project directly. (No copy-paste. No broken formatting.)
3. You compile locally. (No server queue. No waiting.)

When Claude writes your Related Work section, it writes it directly into your `main.tex`.

You watch it happen.
You hit compile.
You see the PDF.

That's it.

**The grunt work vanishes.**

What's left? The actual research.
